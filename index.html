Background: The U.S.-Venezuela Crisis

The political situation in Venezuela has reached a critical juncture following the controversial U.S. intervention in 2025. After years of economic sanctions and diplomatic pressure, the Trump administration authorized a military operation resulting in the capture of President Nicolás Maduro.

Nicolás Maduro

President of Venezuela from 2013 until his capture in 2025. Leader of the United Socialist Party of Venezuela, successor to Hugo Chávez. His presidency was marked by economic crisis, political repression, and disputed elections. Currently detained by U.S. authorities awaiting trial for alleged crimes against humanity and drug trafficking charges.

Donald Trump

47th President of the United States (2017-2021) and 45th President (2025-present). Authorized "Operation Democratic Restoration" in Venezuela in 2025, arguing the need to restore democracy and prevent a humanitarian catastrophe. The operation has divided international opinion, with allies supporting it as necessary intervention and critics condemning it as a violation of sovereignty.

The upcoming Venezuelan election, scheduled for March 2026, will be administered by a joint U.S.-Venezuelan transitional authority. Critics argue this represents a return to "gunboat diplomacy," while supporters claim it's necessary to ensure free and fair elections after decades of authoritarian rule.
